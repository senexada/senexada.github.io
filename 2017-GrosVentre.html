One time before you've seen us in Gros Ventre,
With sentence wrought on sentence, known as prose,
A trick done twice is more than one should want,
So in pentameter this version goes.
(Except the parts co-opted from the sages,
And tailored from their brilliance to our pages.)[1]


When summer's gentle rains have pierced the drought
Of March right to the root, and bathed each sprout
Through every vein with liquid of such power
It brings forth the engendering of the flower;
On pilgrimage then folks desire to start. [2]

Like last time: elevation gain & flowers of every hue

In warm repose upon a flowerly rise,
A bagel feast we ate beneath blue skies.

Scenery, sunshine, and a gentle breeze

The creeks we crossed not once or twice, but more,
(bless'd creeks that keep the glamping throngs away),
Five times the boots unlaced for most of us,
Except for John and I, who, as they say,
Left dusty laces tied and "bombed across."

Cold, clear, refreshing

And then 'twas up and up and up and up,
A weary half a mile into the sky,
With insects swarming at each shady stop,
Til at Shoal Lake we finally arrived.

We stoked the flames to sear the ribeye steak,
Plus spinach, wine, polenta, smores and more,
And with our bellies full we soon retired,
'Neath firmament of shooting stars galore.

Day Two

"Two forks, no brows: the life of Alex M,"
Is how he crisped the bacon on the fire,
The eggs -- all present -- graced the taco shells,
We filled, then drained, then packed, then set out higher.

Past the upper lakes

Idyllic off-trail travel in the upper Gros Ventre valley

With thoughts of Cowtown Pass to Brewster Lake,
We cruised halfway across the speckled hills,
But plans for best eclipse were still to make,
And tradeoffs caused a battle of the wills.

Who won? Who lost? Well, Brewster lost that fight,
And toward an unnamed lake did we alight,
Arrived with time enough until the night,
For Suhas and his pizzas to delight.

Stoking the pizza oven on Skeeter Lake (soon to be Wolf Lake)

What, you expected food pics? We aren't millenials. Washing dishes with waterfall technique.

As dusk arrived and coals were dwindling low,
We heard the plaintive howls round about.
A pack of wolves was roving to and fro,
And one toward us unknowningly set out.

He stopped and turned around with us in sight,
Once kindled he in us a new debate,
About the right technique to use at night,
When wolves around you possibly await.

I tried to join a tent, but stench profound,
Repelled me (with JC) to open ground.

(By dawn I saw that wolves would not have dared,
To breach our camp that snores like sickly bears.)

Day Three

The sun rose on our stationary day,
Delicious cakes we shoveled to our mouth,
Then four set out to summit Darwin Peak,
By going round the north, then east, then south.

Jason atop Darwin summit

Five things 'bout Darwin Peak I'll share with you:
-- The loose, sharp scree that nearly tore my shoe,
-- The stream that disappeared to who-knows-where,
-- The Brewster Three reporting groups down there,
-- The view! The Winds, the Tetons, and our tent,
-- The butt glissade to hasten our descent.

Day Four -- Eclipse Day

Eclipse path through Wymoing

The man who has not music in his soul,
And is not moved by concorde of sweet sounds,
Is fit for treasons, strategems, and spoils, -- [3]
Thus Roger's wholesome soul we know we've found:

From tinny speakers of his iPhone five,
As rays of dawn bid gentle clouds depart,
Did waft the concorde of familiar tune,
About a total eclipse -- of the heart. [4]

Two hours in a blink had passed us by, --
Hypnotic was that peering at the sky --
And yet creation's speech had more to say:
A lessor gift no photo can portray;
The scent of sage and every subtle hue,
In waist-high meadows stretching out of view.

They neither labor nor spin

Uncountable flowers everywhere we turned

The hike 'cross flowered stream beds wasn't far;
A swim we managed in the late-day light;
And then with pesto, whisky, and cigar,
Our final evening passed into the night.

Final notes:

  • Eclipse -- Indescribably cool. Ideal viewing location. Don't miss 2024!
  • Fuel -- used 20oz white gas + 2.25x 8oz isobutanes. Cooked steaks, 4 pizzas, and 2 packs of bacon on the fire. White gas + wind shield is superior in wind.
  • Food -- Bill's new pancke is best yet; pizza was awesome, requires fire, and tongs would help; we are great at open fires; red pepper flakes improved the pesto
  • Crowds -- Saw several groups on day one, two people (miles away) on day two, three day hikers on day three, then no one until the final exit downhill. Parking lot: 12 cars in lot at entry (Fri), 20 at exit (Tue).
  • Route intel -- Day hikers reported 6 hour easy hike from Kinky Creek to Brewster Lake
  • Gear notes -- I'm ready to try 9x9 silny tarp; I need a glove solution; I need to alternate bandanas
  • Footwear -- one thin sock = fail = too sloppy; two thin socks great; bring produce bags for end-of-day drying; trail runners were net win: 5x reduction compared to pack weight, way better on trail, much faster stream crossing (fewer mosquito bites), neutral on most off-trail, worse on loose rock traverses
