Even the hot, dry weekends in the Wind Rivers manage to rain on us. This year lightning ignited the signficant Dollar Lake fire; read on for that story.



The Arrival

I accepted Suhas's offer to arrive early for a spin through Jackson Hole's Via Ferrata. It was a fun course, a beautiful day, and it helped my acclimation. Would do again! It was also an easy drive from Jackson to Pinedale to meet the others.

pre-hike via ferrata





Day One

After breakfast and our third visit of the trip to the Great Outdoor shop (this trip's official favorite store, as documented in our 2023 report), we were driving at 8:06am toward Meadow Lake. Google wasted 20 minutes leading us to a locked gate off road 121; we backtracked to US-191 and the correct turnoff, Burnt Lake Road. The road was good, conditions were dry, and the huge Meadow Lake parking area had two trucks and a trailer (Tue Aug 19).

Meadow Lake parking & trailhead

Pack weights ranged from 22 to 47 pounds: wowzers. (Jeff 22, Alex 34, Bill 39, Steve 43, Roger 46, Suhas 47). The accumulation (decumulation?) of my oddball gear choices through the years is really paying off. I should write a memoir of that journey, including the missteps I reverted, like "no spare underwear."

This year's crew

It was nearly 10am and already 70F when we took the photo and began a couple hours of warm, dry, largely shadeless ascent through occasional young aspen groves. Parts of the greater area burned in the Fayette Lake fire, but this first section of trail was not devastated.

Looking back at Meadow Lake

A bit higher we psased some bleaker fire-scorched sections. The trail was well-cleared through mounds of deadfall It was nice to see new growth of trees reaching 10-20 feet.

Through decades-old damage from the Fayette Lake fire

This is our second western approach to the Winds. Both began with ho-hum scenery and no mountains in sight, until one moment the whole continental divide appears.
