Advice

About problem solving:
  • Pace when pondering hard questions. It works, and it is healthier
  • To deal with a problematic X, apply a bigger X to the problem
  • Don't start hard things after 4pm (starting in mid-30s)
  • Karnaugh map reductions are a valuable simplification technique
About workplace issues:
  • Flush resumes with multiple <2 year items: lack perserverance or were fired.
  • Ask interviewees about hobbies and accomplishments and metrics
  • Bill: just stop doing stuff you dislike
About health:
  • Walk as many hills and stairs as possible, all life long.
  • Do two minute max intensity every day
About life in general:
  • Simplify
  • Only change one thing at a time
  • You will get more of whatever you automate (e.g. autopay --> spend more money)
  • When overwhelmed, identify the worst thorn and remove it. Iterate
  • Motivation is easiest within a committed group
  • Catch all negative self talk, stop, and deliberately turn to something positive
  • Avoid taxes by not realizing income
  • Marginal cost analysis is dangerous: each step can be rational, but end result is far worse
  • Finish with quality: the most important 3 ski turns are the last 3 before the lift
  • Do creative work in the morning before inputs make you reactive
