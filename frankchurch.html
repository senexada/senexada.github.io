Frank Church Wilderness Research

The Frank Church area is so large it is covered by two huge double-sided maps at 1:100k. I have the north half map, which is mostly forested ridges.

The most popular area in the northern section is the craggy above-treeline peaks and lakes of the Bighorn Crags. Image searches show beautiful photos of the area, but it has some drawbacks:

  • ++ scenic area
  • -- only one reasonable route in & out
  • -- it is the most heavily traveled area in the Frank Church
  • -- most of the lakes are within a day hike of the trailhead (ex: this report says airplane lake is 8-10 miles)

Another famous area is Chamberlain Basin, which has no craggy peaks, but has some impressive reports online.
