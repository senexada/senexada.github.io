Until the 20th century it was customary for active men to walk 30 miles a day in rugged areas. Evidence is found in realistic fiction, such as Jack London's short story xyz, in which the main character travels 30 miles a day on foot through snow in the Yukon territory. It is also found in a variety of nonfiction accounts. In Hind's Exploration up the Moisie River (in Quebec), he describes snow-capped peaks "distant two days' journey in snow-shoes (about 60 miles)". The journals of Lewis and Clark related a discussion on Saturday July 26th, 1806, in which the Minnetares tribe "informed us that from hence to the establishment where they trade on the Suskasawan river is only 6 days easy march or such as they usually travel with their women and childred which may be estimated at about 150 ms."
