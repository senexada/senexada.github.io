Ansel Adams Wilderness
September 2015

After the prior year's rigor and drenching in Wyoming, we returned to Cali for a drier, gentler trip. It was indeed bone dry but its gentleness depends on your perspective (more on that later). It also offered a backcountry surprise, something I never expected to stumble upon in the wilderness.

Mammoth Lakes is a beautiful mountain town high in the Sierra wilderness, yet trailhead access is tedious. In summertime, the San Joaquin trailheads are only accessible via a slow shuttle bus from the gondola. We parked near the gondola about 10am, boarded a shuttle at 10:40, reached the Devil's Postpile stop (7560') at 11:15, and hit the John Muir Trail (JMT) at 11:30.

The first mile reminded us that we were indeed back in California: saturating sunshine, rare shade, cloudless but hazy-from-wildfire skies, and the finest of fine Sierra trail dust.

It begins: sun and sierra trail dust

We reached the Superior Lake trail in 45 minutes and began the steady, moderate, and delightfully shady climb westward. We reached Beck's cabin (9100') three hours later, which included a 45 minute lunch stop. From Beck's cabin it took half hour to the foot of Superior Lake (notably: the first usable water since the trailhead) and another hour to our destination, the north shore of Beck Lake.

Campsite one: Beck Lake

It is a beautiful spot, isn't it? But the photo doesn't capture the chill. At sunset it was a comfortable-sounding 50 degrees, but steady winds above 20mph sucked away all our heat. Roger was shivering, possibly due to preexisting weariness from his half-ironman. Steve had already used six pair of socks and was hurting, a bad omen for the morrow.

Our classic first night meal was perfectly proportioned this time: 6 steaks, 2 bricks of spinach, 1 loaf of polenta, and 4 key lime pies (for six people). There were dissenting opinions on bringing N-1 steaks on future trips. The pan fry worked well, but grease cleanup was ugly. We need to stick with fire-permitted first night locations, which excludes most of Cali.

The sun set at 6:15pm and the Milky Way's structure was visible by 8:35. We slept early to escape the howling wind.

close with gentleness comment: gentle measured by mileage; not gentle by our condition

Final notes for the archives:

  • Avoid mandatory shuttles -- can add several hours
