Death

Meditations on death

1. Jesus wept

The shortest verse of scripture.

God Himself -- omnipotent, omniscient, eternal -- felt physical pain of loss, of mourning. He has felt all that we have felt.

2. Death diminishes me

When [the church] burries a Man, that action concerns me: All mankind is of one Author, and is one volume. No man is an Island, entire of itself; every man is a piece of the Continent, a part of the main; if a Clod be washed away by the Sea, Europe is the less. Any man's death diminishes me because I am involved in Mankind; and therefore never send to know for whom the bell tolls; It tolls for thee.
John Donne, Devotions XVII

3. Death of a loved one

My heart was black with grief. Whatever I looked upon had the air of death. They could not say 'He will come soon,' as they would in his life when he was absent. I became a great enigma to myself and was forever asking my soul why it was sad and why it disquieted me so sorely. And my soul knew not what to answer me.
St Augustine, Confessions IV
I hope he will not suffer so much [the loss of his wife] as I yet suffer for the loss of mine. I have ever since seemed to myself broken off from mankind; a kind of solitary wanderer in the wild of life, without any direction, or fixed point of view: a gloomy gazer on the world to which I have little relation.
Dr Johnson, letter of Dec 1754
There is a sort of an invisible blanket between the world and me. I find it hard to take in what anyone says. Or perhaps, hard to want to take it in. It is so uninteresting.
CS Lewis, A Grief Observed

4. Inevitability

With regard to the sharpest and most melting sorrow, that which arises from the loss of those whom we have loved with tenderness, it may be observed, that friendship between mortals can be contracted on no other terms that that one must some time mourn for the other's death.
Dr Johnson, Rambler XVII

5. We consider our coming death

And whomsoever these Bells bring to the ground today, if he and I had been compared yesterday, perchance I should have been thought likelier to come to this preferment than he.

Now, this Bell tolling softly for another, says to me, Thou must die.
John Donne, Devotion XVI

We are born to die. Death reveals our smallness and fragility.

When I consider the short duration of my life, swallowed up in the eternity before and after, the little space which I fill, and even can see, engulfed in the infinite immensity of spaces of which I am ignorant, and which knows me not, I am frightened. The eternal silence of these infinite spaces frightens me.
Pascal, Pensees 205
And solemn before us
Veiled, the dark Portal,
Goal of all mortal; -
Stars silent rest o'er us,
Graves under us silent.

While earnest thou gazest
Comes boding of terror,
Comes phantasm and error,
Perplexes the bravest
With doubt and misgiving.
Goethe, The Mason
For what is your life? It is even a vapor, that appeareth for a little time, and then vanish away
James 4

6. The Tragedy

A Comedy ends with happily ever after. A Tragedy ends with death. If death is the end, every life is a Tragedy.

The last act is tragic, however happy all the rest of the play is; at the last a little earth is thrown upon our head, and that is the end forever.
Pascal, Pensees 210

Death "puts an end to all our schemes, deprives us of all our posessions," and begins the dissolution of all we have ever thought, done, or been. All turns to dust, everyone we know, everyone they will know, ad infinitum.

Vanity of vanities -- all is vanity. That which befalls the sons of men befalls the beasts; as the one dies, so dies the other. All are of the dust, and all turn to dust again.
Ecclesiastes 3
Out out, brief candle!
Life's but a walking shadow, a poor player
That struts and frets his hour upon the stage
And then is heard no more: it is a tale
Told by an idiot, full of sound and fury
Signifying nothing
Shakespeare, Macbeth V

7. The Comedy

A Comedy ends with happily ever after --
which requires 'ever,' i.e. eternity.

He has put eternity into the hearts of men.
Ecclesiastes 3

God is the transcendant source of being itself, unconstrained by time.

If they ask me 'What is his name?' what am I to tell them?
God replied 'I am WHO AM'
Exodus 3
Before Abraham was, I AM
John 8
I am the Alpha and the Omega, the One who is and who was and who is to come, the Almighty
Revelation 1

God invites us into his eternal being

Surely this day you will be with me in paradise.
Luke 23

8. Why pain and sorrow?

Mystery. We see through the glass darkly.

Job lost his children and his health, he asked God why, and God replied with a non-answer.

God comes in at the end, not to answer riddles, but to propound them. Verbally speaking the enigmas of Jehovah seem darker and more desolate than the enigmas of Job; yet Job was comfortless before the speech of Jehovah and is comforted after it. The riddles of God are more satisfying than the solutions of man.
Chesterton, Introduction to Job

It can point to eternal things.

No man hath affliction enough that is not matured, and ripened by it, and made fit for God by that affliction. By this consideration of another's danger, I take mine own into contemplation, and so secure myself, by making my recourse to my God, who is our only security
John Donne, Devotion XVII
Oh Lord, teach us to keep in mind that we must die, so that we become wise.
Bach, BWV 106

9. Fear not

The commonest command of scripture. A thousand years before Christ, amidst death and illness:

I know that my redeemer liveth, and that he shall stand at the latter day upon on the earth: And though after my skin worms destory this body, yet in my flesh shall I see God: Whom I shall see for myself, and mine eyes shall behold
Job 19

Jesus:

In the world you will have tribulation, but be of good cheer, for I have overcome the world.
John 16

10. If I doubt?

What is more difficult, to be born or to rise again; that that which has never been should be, or that what has been should be again? Is it more difficult to come into existence than to return to it?
Pascal, Pensees 222

11. How does faith look?

Bach, from various sources:

God's timing is the very best timing.
In him we live, move, and exist,
as long as he wills.
In him we die at the right time,
when he wills.

Ah, Lord, teach us to keep in mind
that we must die,
so that we become wise.

Put your house in order,
for you are going to die
and will not keep on living.

This is the old covenant:
Man, you must die!
Yes, come, Lord Jesus, come!

Into your hands I commit my spirit;
you have redeemed me, Lord,
you faithful God.

Today you will be with me in Paradise.

In peace and joy I depart
According to God's will;
At ease is my hear and mind,
Calm and peaceful,
As God has promised me.
Death has become my sleep.

Glory, praise, honor, and majesty
Be prepared for you, God the Father and Son,
The Holy Spirit, by name!
May your divine power
Make us victorious
Through Jesus Christ. Amen.

Bach, Gottes Zeit ist die allebeste Zeit (BWV 106)
