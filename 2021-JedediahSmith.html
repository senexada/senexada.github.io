Jedediah Smith Wilderness
June 2021


We hit the west slope of the Tetons in June, rather than our typical August, minus some usual suspects, who had been replaced by impressionable minors. The result of bringing teenagers: "would do again."

Thunderstorms disrupted the delicate Denver airport, causing us to arrive in Idaho Falls at 2am lacking two essential bags. Instead of driving for the hills on Saturday morning, we sat in the hotel lobby brainstorming the minimal gear required to salvage the trip. Thankfully, the missing bags arrived mid morning and we were driving by noon, crossing the planes of Idaho toward the western Tetons.





Day One

We dropped one car at our exit, North Leigh trailhead, which is about 20 minutes off the pavement of Idaho 33. On this particular Saturday afternoon (June 26) there were eight cars at the trailhead and one horse trailer. The seven of us crammed into the canyonero for the hour drive to Coyote Meadows.

The crew, minus our narrator

My initial impression from the dozen horse trailers at Coyote Creek was "uh oh, crowds." But some groups were already exiting as we began (at 3:10pm), and more crossed our path during the first hour. After that, we were alone.

3pm start from Coyote Meadows

The hike to Corral Basin was easy, with only two modest uphill climbs. A 12-horse commercial group passed us outbound at the bridge over North Bitch Creek. The forest opened to views of mountains after the halfway point.

Approaching Corral Basin

Reaching the foot of Corral Basin at 7:40pm, we took the first site after the sign (which was ultimately the only obvious site we saw in the Basin). The fire was roaring in an hour and our late hamburger dinner spanned the hour after that. Nathan got a chill that persisted despite a 15 degree sleeping bag on a warm evening. My watch showed 28k steps for the day.

Day Two

I awoke to bright daylight at 5:30 and just lazed. Everyone was up by 7:50, the egg tacos were consumed by 9:00 (raw onion is heavy but so good), and we were packed and walking by 10:20. Nathan, thankfully, felt good as new. Three people on five hourses passed us outbound; they had camped in the basin last night somewhere out of sight, and were the last people we would see for the next 2+ days.

In the first half hour we passed the Camp Lake junction, which showed 4.3 miles to our pass. Immediately we were slowed by deadfall and the first shoes-off crossing. We continued up the meadows of the unnamed valley toward Dead Horse Pass.

Betwixt Corral Basin and Dead Horse Pass

We ate lunch beside the second shoes-off crossing, this one about knee deep. The next 2.5 hours were steady, shaded switchbacks, frequently hopping streams while hearing the muffled roar of always-nearby rapids. At 8400' snow appeared and trail detection became erratic due to the snow pack. We took several breaks en route and reached the snowy pass just after 4pm.

"Is this the trail?" "Probably."

Dead Horse Pass, ~9500'

Fine spot for a rest. Dead Horse Pass

The south side of the ridge, unsurprisingy, had less snow -- barely any -- and a good switchbacked trail took us quickly down to the river, where we scouted and chose the site at the junction.

Find Nathan. (May need to click for high res)

Our site was in the trees and by dusk was thick with mosquitos. See the end of this report for notes on improved pasta logistics. The offspring did admirably on the first full day with legit uphill, and on cooking & cleaning. Gadget showed 23k steps. The evening was warm.

Day Three

True darkness lasted only from about 10pm to 5am. Another beautiful day in the mountains, another two hours of teenager-sized batches of pancakes (note: no one eats the walnuts). Our typical slow start lent time for the teenages to achieve a level 2 bridge across Badger Creek.

Level 2 bridge over Badger Creek

We began hiking in earnest about noon. The first few hours had surprisingly steep sections for a stock trail, and got warm, as it was our first day without shade or clouds.

Trail 008 near Crow's Nest

We ate at the false pass near Crow's Nest, then continued to traverse under the ridgeline, losing the trail in the swampy basins.

Highlands along Trail 008, toward Green Lake

At an opportune spot (near the 4,854k N UTC grid line), we dropped packs for a ten minute side trip into Grand Teton park, which offered excellent photo ops of Grand Teton. The unnamed peak 10,538 east of Littles Peak looks to be a spectacular spot to capture the full relief of Grand Teton on some future golden hour. It also looks alluring to do an exit hike down Leigh Canyon.

Tetons

Tetons sans people

Leigh Valley is also quite scenic

Unable to find the trail in the boggy hollow, we lost about half hour on false trails before finding a true junction and reaching our unnamed lake (the highest lake draining into Green Lake) at 6pm. This was our best site: glorious oranges, purples, and pinks of sunset while the kids skipped stones onto a serene lake. It was a magical evening.

Day Four

Thus far the scenery had improved daily (somewhat expected, given the low altitude forested start), and today would continue the excellence of day three.

Toward Green Lake

We first passed a pretty unnamed lake with some good sites, then some small cascades interspersed with clear pools. We then passed the aptly named Green Lake, its surface reflecting the verdant slope on its western side, and which flows out through a beaver dam.

No shoes off for this stream crossing (Green Lake exit)

The next section had stunning views along and over the ridge into the Tin Cup Creek watershed.

Compulsory walking shot

Perfectly framed vista of North Leigh Creek

After topping out, there was a delightful meadow walk while looking down a vertical mile across green meadows and wooded ridges to the plains of Idaho. There was a light breeze and barely a cloud in the sky. This was a wonderful section to stroll down.

Loving it on trail 019

Perfect day

Prior to this stretch, we passed water at least every hour (of the entire trip). This section had none. The trail dust was finer at low altitudes and the trail spanned a lot of vertical. We passed a family and two day hikers coming inbound, the first people since Corral Basin. Reaching the river & road, 5 cars were parked; another five minutes down the road was our car in the larger lot, alone.

Retrospective

This was a solid showing for our first trip with teenagers. I enjoyed the June timeframe: copious daylight, lots of active streams, and no wildfires. We possibly got lucky with warm evenings. Trail-finding was harder than August due to snowpack.

The final campsite was by far the best, buttressing our previous bias toward lakes over meadows and streams. Valley bottom streams have heavy mosquitos and are less scenic.

Mileage and elevations were good. The last two days were most scenic. It was good to do the final stretch downhill rather than uphill.

The unnamed peak (mentioned in the Teton NP episode) looks to be a unique, spectacular spot to view and photograph Grand Teton. It may be worth a return trip, hiking out Mink Lake through the National Park. The Teton Crest route also looks appealing. Some related Teton Crest links:

Fuel usage: 7 people, half of one dinner (hamburgers) on fire, used 22 oz (2.75 x 8oz canisters) isobutane.

Food: 7 people

  • hamburger dinner -- 12x5.5oz patties, 12 thin buns, 16 slices cheese, 7 cookies. Forgot ketchup + mustard [filling, no leftovers]
  • breakfast tacos -- 14 small eggs, 1 lb bacon, 14 tortilla, 8oz cheese, 2oz hot sauce, small onion, 2oz oil, salt + pepper [on the light side]
  • lunch charcuterie -- salami 8oz, calabrese 8oz, prosciuto 2x3oz, gruyere 6oz, cheddar 7oz, 2x full sandwich sized ziplocs of crackers [always looks small but very filling; 4-5oz meat leftover]
  • pasta dinner -- 2 lbs macaroni, 2x7oz pesto, 3oz parmesan, 3 chocolate bars [kids could have eaten more; 3.7L pot holds 0.66lbs pasta; subdivide servings ahead; takes 15min/batch]
  • Pancake breakfast -- Bill's usual. Not sure of amounts [had plenty; walnuts leftover]
  • PB & J lunch -- 8 pitas, 18oz PB, 18oz jam, 1 can pringles, 12 small snickers [good amount]
  • Jambalaya dinner -- 2x12oz zatarains, 5x3oz chicken, 2x10oz summer sausage, 4oz olive oil, 3 chocolate bars [kids could have eaten more; cutting takes a while; 5min boil + 25min simmer per batch]
  • Oatmeal breakfast -- didn't record details. Probably a few instant packets each
  • Snacks -- ate all snacks; for N each day, bring 3x honey stingers and jerky and bar
