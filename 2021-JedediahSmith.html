Jedediah Smith Wilderness
June 2021


We hit the west slope of the Tetons in June, rather than our typical August, and without many of our usual suspects, who had been replaced by impressionable minors. The result of brining teenagers: "would do again."

We began slowly, with thunderstorms disrupting the delicate Denver airport. We arrived Idaho Falls at 2am without two essential bags. Instead of driving for the hills on Saturday morning, we sat in the hotel lobby brainstorming the minimal set of gear we would need to locate & purchase to rescue the trip. But thankfully, the missing bags arrived mid morning and we were driving by noon, crossing the planes of Idaho toward the western Tetons.





Day One

We dropped one car at our exit, North Leigh trailhead, which is about 20 minutes off the pavement of Idaho 33. On this particular Saturday afternoon (June 26) there were eight cars at the trailhead and one horse trailer. The seven of us crammed into the canyonero for the hour drive to Coyote Meadows.

My initial impression from the dozen horse trailers at Coyote Creek was "uh oh, crowds." But some groups were already exiting by our hiking start (3:10pm), and more crossed our path during the first hour. After that, we were alone.

It was an easy hike to Corral Basin: mostly flat, with only two modest uphill climbs. The initial miles are forested, and meadow, with the mountains coming into view only after the halfway point.
