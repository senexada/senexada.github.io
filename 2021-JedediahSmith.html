Jedediah Smith Wilderness
June 2021


We hit the west slope of the Tetons in June, rather than our typical August, minus some usual suspects, who had been replaced by impressionable minors. The result of brining teenagers: "would do again."

Thunderstorms disrupted the delicate Denver airport, causing us to arrive in Idaho Falls at 2am lacking two essential bags. Instead of driving for the hills on Saturday morning, we sat in the hotel lobby brainstorming the minimal gear required to salvage the trip. Thankfully, the missing bags arrived mid morning and we were driving by noon, crossing the planes of Idaho toward the western Tetons.





Day One

We dropped one car at our exit, North Leigh trailhead, which is about 20 minutes off the pavement of Idaho 33. On this particular Saturday afternoon (June 26) there were eight cars at the trailhead and one horse trailer. The seven of us crammed into the canyonero for the hour drive to Coyote Meadows.

The crew, minus our narrator

My initial impression from the dozen horse trailers at Coyote Creek was "uh oh, crowds." But some groups were already exiting as we began (at 3:10pm), and more crossed our path during the first hour. After that, we were alone.

3pm start from Coyote Meadows

The hike to Corral Basin was easy, with only two modest uphill climbs. A 12-horse commercial group passed us outbound at the bridge over North Bitch Creek. The forest opened to views of mountains after the halfway point.

Approaching Corral Basin

Reaching the foot of Corral Basin at 7:40pm, we took the first site after the sign (which was ultimately the only obvious sight we saw in the Basin). The fire was roaring in an hour and our late dinner spanned the hour after that. Nathan got a chill that persisted in the 15 degree bad on a warm evening. My watch showed 28k steps for the day.
