An Honest Look at Private Equity Advantages: - Access to small, fast growing companies that is difficult to find elsewhere - Majority control, which allows tighter management feedback loops (think Buffett) - Perhaps (?) uncorrelated with public equities - Access to non-recourse leverage - Structural earnings multiple step up (small to mid is consistent step-up) - Investing in bread-and-butter American small businesses Disadvantages: - Cannot meaningfully measure performance for many years - Capital calls at arbitrary times require liquidity and frequent email access - Capital is called over many years, which aggravates the intermediate liquidity & investment situation - Typically less than committed capital is called, but no guarantee - Performance fee is based on IRR, which somewhat misaligns mgmt & investors (ex: sell after 6 months at +30% or after 12 months at +50%. Mgmt will sell after 6) - K1 tax complications - typically arrive after 4/15 - can include state-source income, requiring a tax return in those states - K1 accounting is complex and it's hard to tell if you're being treated fairly - You lose all timing over taxable income and type - The 2% mgmt fee is not tax deductable, but gains are fully taxed, which creates a super-tax regime - The private equity space is very competitve, increasing the risk of overpaying or adverse selection - The fund's success depends a lot on the team, but the team can be fragile due to back loaded comp (good people may leave early, which is highly disruptive, if they anticipate a small performance fee)
