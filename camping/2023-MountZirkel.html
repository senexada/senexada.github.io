Mt Zirkel CO
Sep 2023


Another Labor Day weekend, another trip to the east slopes of the Mt Zirkel wilderness.



September 1, 2023 -- this year we stayed in Silverthorne, which was a good trade vs Walden: earlier night, better hotel, more breakfast and shopping options.

Leaving the hotel at 9:08am, we reached the forest boundary in 2h15 and soon saw forest road 650, which looked like a reasonable option on the map, but even the first few hundred yards were too dicey for a rental SUV. We abanodoned that idea without hesitation.

Midday on a Friday of Labor Day, there were three cars at North Fork, zero at South Fork. I somehow forgot the eggs -- doh. We were walking by 12:30 through the biggest aspens I've seen.

Big aspens

In the first two hours we traversed through aspen, hopped across the first live water (about an hour in), stopped for lunch, and passed two backpackers near an old cabin. And we found coveted wild raspberry bushes along the creek & rockslide area.

Up Bear Creek valley

Partway up the valley we chose a direct bushwacking route to Bear Lake. We departed the trail near the stream draining Bear Lake and hiked an hour through challenging steep underbrush in prime bear territory. Upon reaching the lake, we pre dug some holes, filtered water, and collected wood, but were too hungry to wait for coals; so we pan fried the burgers. Potato bun would have been even better. Pickles were great.

Off trail to Bear Lake

We faced some big wind gusts and spurts of rain during cleanup, but nothing drenching. The gusts & the dead pines made it challenging to find safe tent sites.

Day Two

Sat morning -- We woke to beautiful reds on the mountain tops and Watson already fishing. A solo backpacker passed us on the way out; he was presumably there last night but we hadn't seen him (or anyone). My notes say we "redeemed the loss of the eggs" -- but the notes don't say how! Maybe we sizzled the lunch ham.

Morning on Bear Lake

Today's activity was a day hike to the continental divide, with frequent stops for marmot and pika viewing. Watson had infinite capacity for pika viewing.

Pika

We had great views down to North Park and over the divide into the western Zirkel wilderness.

Ute Pass looking east

Another view from Ute Pass

On the way down I scouted the next valley north, but the topography was such that I couldn't see any lakes by my turnaround time.

The afternoon swim was superb, though Patton said it gave him "hypothermia times three." I saw another 5 people and 2 dogs in our vicinity. We almost finished our first fuel canister while cooking the mac and cheese.

Day Three

We made Swiss Miss and executed the two-hour pancake breakfast. At 10am we began bombardment of the flotilla on the lake. What Watson deemed "enhanced construction" meant the flotilla was "still legally alive" even as they were "running out of rocks."

Let the bombardment begin

Enjoying a great spot

We walked around the lake, which required some nice boulder scrambles.

Scrambling around Bear Lake

Then we endured a couple brief, violent rainfalls and a snowfall. It was fun to sit under the tarp and watch.

Snowfall

And afterward, the rainbow

Bill tended a fire from noon til 9pm. We cooked dinner and slept.





Day Four

Awake at 6am, we were walking by 7:45 (within 15 minutes of our target). We stayed fully on trail through the upper meadows:

Trail between Bear Lakes and Ute Pass

We retraced the trail down valley, then back through the big aspens on a blustery fall day with deep shadows alternating with bright sunshine. It was a beautiful experience for all the senses.

Big aspens with great shading

We were back to the car before noon and on our way to Silverthorne for Chipotle.

Success!

Retrospective

This was a longer hike than our prior Mt Zirkel trip (to Rainbow Lake), and our off-trail shortcut increased the degree of difficulty. (The off-trail section was steep with lots of underbrush and deadfall). The aspen grove at the start was fabulous. The day hike to the continental divide was straightforward and its views were good in all directions.
